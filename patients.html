Patient Resources

We provide educational materials, recovery guides, and support for patients undergoing orthopedic procedures. Learn what to expect before, during, and after your surgery.

1. Knee and Hip Joint at a Glance

The knee and hip joints are major weight-bearing joints in the body that enable movement and flexibility. These joints are lined with cartilage that helps in smooth motion and shock absorption.

2. What is Arthritis?

Arthritis refers to inflammation of one or more joints, causing pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Over time, it can lead to joint damage and loss of function.

3. Top 3 Arthritis Types Causing Joint Deterioration

  • Osteoarthritis (OA)
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
  • Post-Traumatic Arthritis

4. Commonly Affected Joints and Their Symptoms

Knees, hips, hands, and spine are commonly affected. Symptoms include pain, swelling, stiffness, reduced range of motion, and joint deformity in severe cases.

5. Treatment Options

  • Medications (NSAIDs, corticosteroids)
  • Physical therapy
  • Assistive devices (canes, braces)
  • Minimally invasive procedures
  • Joint replacement surgery

6. Why Should You Consider Joint Replacement Surgery?

If pain severely impacts your quality of life and conservative treatments no longer provide relief, joint replacement surgery may restore mobility and function.

7. Benefits of TKR/THR

  • Reduced joint pain
  • Improved mobility
  • Enhanced quality of life
  • Long-term joint durability
